Description-based Controllable Text-to-Speech with Cross-Lingual Voice Control

Ryuichi Yamamoto, Yuma Shirahata, Masaya Kawamura, Kentaro Tachibana
Sep 5, 2024

Submitted to ICASSP 2025

Abstract

We propose a novel description-based controllable text-to-speech (TTS) method that eliminates the need for audio-description pairs in the target language. To address the lack of labeled paired data, we combine a TTS model trained on the target language with a description control model trained on another language. These models share disentangled timbre and style representations based on self-supervised learning (SSL), allowing for disentangled voice control, such as controlling speaking styles while retaining the original timbre. Furthermore, because the SSL-based timbre and style representations are language-agnostic, combining the TTS and control models while sharing the same embedding space effectively enables cross-lingual control of voice characteristics. Experiments on English and Japanese TTS demonstrate that our method achieves high naturalness and controllability for both languages, even though no audio-description pairs are used for Japanese.
